🔥 How L1 and L2 trade “original coins” even when deposits/withdrawals happen on L2

How does BaseETH trade as if it’s ETH, even though deposits/withdrawals happen through Base, not Ethereum L1?

This is the same pattern used by:

---

🧩 THE CORE TRUTH

L2 coins are not the original L1 coins.

But the system makes them behave like the original by using:

1. Canonical Bridge

2. Canonical Mapping

3. Liquidity Sync

4. Withdrawal Finality

Let’s go layer by layer.

---

🏛️ 1. L1 = The Source of Truth

Ethereum L1 is the canonical ledger for ETH.

Base, Arbitrum, Optimism, etc. do not mint ETH.

Instead:

When you deposit ETH to Base:

• You send real ETH to the Base Canonical Bridge Contract on L1.
• That contract locks your ETH.
• Base L2 mints BaseETH (an IOU representing your locked ETH).


So BaseETH is:

BaseETH = Claim on locked ETH in L1 bridge contract


This is why BaseETH trades 1:1 with ETH.

---

🧱 2. L2 = The Execution Layer

On Base, users trade BaseETH, not real ETH.

But BaseETH is treated as “ETH” because:

• It has the same symbol (ETH)
• It has the same decimals
• It is the canonical representation of ETH on Base
• All DEXs on Base use BaseETH as the native asset


So when you trade on Base:

You trade BaseETH ↔ tokens


Not L1 ETH.

But because BaseETH is redeemable 1:1 for ETH, the market treats it as ETH.

---

🔄 3. How Trading Works

Trading on L2 uses L2 balances only

When you swap on Base:

• You use BaseETH
• The AMM pools hold BaseETH + tokens
• No L1 interaction happens
• No real ETH moves on L1


This is why L2 trades are cheap and fast.

---

🏦 4. Withdrawal Logic

When you withdraw BaseETH → ETH:

1. You burn BaseETH on Base.
2. Base posts a proof to Ethereum L1.
3. The L1 bridge contract releases your locked ETH.


So the flow is:

Deposit: L1 ETH → Locked → Mint BaseETH
Trade: BaseETH ↔ tokens (L2 only)
Withdraw: Burn BaseETH → Unlock L1 ETH


This is the entire logic.

---

🧠 WHY IT FEELS LIKE “TRADING REAL ETH”

Because:

• BaseETH is always redeemable for ETH
• Arbitrage bots keep BaseETH = ETH price
• DEXs treat BaseETH as the native asset
• Wallets show “ETH” even though it’s BaseETH


So the illusion is perfect.
