1. Where prices come from if you don’t use CMC/CG

You have three native sources of truth:

1. Your own orderbooks / AMM pools• Best bid/ask = spot price
• Pool reserves = implied price

2. Your own trades• Last traded price
• VWAP over a window

3. External reference feeds (optional, but not CMC/CG)• Direct CEX APIs (Binance, OKX, etc.)
• Oracles (Chainlink, Pyth, etc.) if you want on‑chain reference



You combine these into your own price oracle, exposed via your API.

---

2. Core logic: how your exchange “runs its own price”

For each market (e.g. BSV/USDT):

1. From orderbook (orderbook‑driven):• bestBid = highest bid
• bestAsk = lowest ask
• midPrice = (bestBid + bestAsk) / 2

2. From AMM pool (pool‑driven):• For pool with reserves R_base, R_quote:price = \frac{R_{quote}}{R_{base}}


3. From trades (trade‑driven):• lastPrice = price of most recent trade
• vwap = Σ(price * volume) / Σ(volume) over last N minutes



You can choose:

• Display price = lastPrice
• Fallback = midPrice or pool price if no recent trades


All of this is your own data, no external API.

3. Your own price API (no CMC/CG)

Expose a simple endpoint:

GET /v1/ticker?symbol=BSVUSDT

Return:

{

This is now your sovereign price feed.

4. Candles without CoinGecko

You already have everything needed to build candles yourself:

1. Every trade has:• price
• amount
• timestamp
• symbol

2. For each interval (1m, 5m, 1h, etc.), you aggregate:• open = first trade price in interval
• high = max price
• low = min price
• close = last trade price
• volume = sum of traded base or quote



Store in DB (e.g. Timescale/Postgres) and serve via:

GET /v1/candles?symbol=BSVUSDT&interval=1h&from=...&to=...

Return:

[
  { "t": 1710000000, "o": 13.9, "h": 14.1, "l": 13.8, "c": 13.82, "v": 1023.5 },
  ...
]


Again: all from your own trades, no external API.

---

5. Feeding your own prices into TradingView

TradingView doesn’t care where prices come from.
It only cares that your datafeed API implements their contract.

You already have:

• /v1/candles → maps to TradingView /history
• /v1/ticker → can support real‑time updates
• /v1/markets → maps to /symbols and /search


So your TradingView datafeed service does:

• GET /symbols?symbol=BSVUSDT → uses your /markets
• GET /history?symbol=BSVUSDT... → uses your /candles
• Live updates → subscribe to your WebSocket (ticker, candles)


No CoinGecko. No CMC.
Just Orah → TradingView.

---

6. Making it robust without external price APIs

To avoid weird prices on illiquid pairs:

• Use liquidity‑weighted logic:• If orderbook is thin, lean more on:• VWAP over last N minutes
• Or even a blended price with a CEX reference (if you choose)


• Guardrails:• Reject trades that move price > X% in one tick (anti‑manipulation)
• Cap displayed price movement per interval if volume is tiny



You can still be independent while optionally using CEX feeds as sanity checks, not as your primary oracle.

7. In one sentence

You run your own price engine by:

• Using your own orderbooks, pools, and trades
• Aggregating them into tickers and candles
• Exposing them via your own API
• Wiring that API into TradingView’s datafeed


No CoinGecko. No CoinMarketCap.
Just Orah as a sovereign source of truth.
