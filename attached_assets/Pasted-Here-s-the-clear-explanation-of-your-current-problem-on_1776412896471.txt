Why “Only trade wallet balance deducted, not working”
You have two completely separate balances:
	1	On-chain wallet balance (small amount, e.g. ~0.010 ETH labeled “On-chain (ETH) — not tradable”):
	◦	This is your real ETH sitting in your connected wallet.
	◦	Trading does NOT deduct from here automatically. That’s why you see “wallet balance not deducted” when you place orders.
	2	OrahDEX Exchange / Internal balance (the big numbers you see in Portfolio — ETH 2,949, USDC 2,596, USDT 13k+, BSV 154, etc.):
	◦	This is like a CEX-style account inside OrahDEX.
	◦	When you click Sell ETH (or Buy), the platform instantly debits ETH from this internal balance and credits USDC (or whatever) to it.
	◦	No wallet signature is required for the trade itself — that’s why “no sign transaction” happens.
	◦	Your filled orders (the “Sell Order Filled!” notifications) only moved numbers inside OrahDEX, not on-chain.
This hybrid design (off-chain order book matching + on-chain deposit/withdrawal) lets trades feel fast and cheap, but it creates exactly the confusion you’re having: trading only affects the internal “trade balance”, never your real wallet unless you move funds manually.
How to Make Trading Actually Deduct from Your Wallet (If That’s What You Want)
If you want trades to pull directly from (and deduct from) your real wallet balance, you must first deposit/bridge funds from on-chain → OrahDEX Exchange balance:
	1	Go to the Portfolio tab (or Bridge tab).
	2	Find your on-chain ETH (the small “not tradable” one).
	3	Click Deposit or Bridge next to it.
	4	Your wallet will pop up — this is the step that requires you to sign the transaction and pay any gas/fee.
	5	Once the deposit confirms on-chain, the amount moves to your OrahDEX Exchange balance and becomes tradable.
	6	Now place a sell/buy order — it will deduct from the (now larger) internal balance.
To reverse it (get money back to your real wallet):
	•	In Portfolio → OrahDEX Exchange section, click Withdraw next to the asset.
	•	Confirm the withdrawal in your wallet (another sign + on-chain tx).
