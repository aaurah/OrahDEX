1. Sentence‑Level Clarity — Remove Ambiguity

Examples of ambiguous phrasing found in the document:

• “The protocol is relevant to all of them, whether they wish it or not.”
• “The cold-start problem is solved at the protocol level.”
• “The protocol is architecturally incapable of custody.”


These are strong statements — but they must be tightened.

Corrected versions:

• “The protocol applies equally to all participants because it is permissionless and globally accessible.”
• “The cold-start liquidity problem is eliminated through the Genesis Liquidity Engine’s permanent bonding curve.”
• “The protocol cannot hold user funds because no contract or server has the ability to take custody.”


This preserves strength while improving precision.

---

2. Remove Redundant Adjectives

Examples:

• “sovereign, permissionless, non-custodial trading protocol”
• “immutable, permanent, tamper-evident OP_RETURN proofs”


These are correct but occasionally repetitive.

Corrected:

• “sovereign, non-custodial trading protocol”
• “immutable OP_RETURN settlement proofs”


Precision > repetition.

---

3. Replace Passive Voice with Deterministic Voice

Examples:

• “Trades are settled on BSV.”
• “Liquidity is provided by AMM pools.”


Corrected:

• “BSV settles all trades.”
• “AMM pools provide liquidity.”


This strengthens the sovereign tone.

---

4. Ensure Every Paragraph Has One Purpose

Some paragraphs mix:

• explanation
• justification
• marketing
• technical detail


These must be separated.

Example correction:

❌
“The Genesis Liquidity Engine guarantees liquidity for every asset and eliminates the cold-start problem that has plagued every DEX.”

✅
Paragraph 1 — What it is:
“The Genesis Liquidity Engine is a virtual AMM that provides permanent, simulation-based liquidity for all listed assets.”

Paragraph 2 — What it solves:
“It eliminates the cold-start liquidity problem by ensuring every asset is tradeable from the moment it is listed.”

6. Ensure Every Section Ends with a Summary

Example:

7. Remove All Unnecessary Transitional Phrases

Examples:

• “In other words…”
• “Put simply…”
• “It is important to note that…”


These weaken the sovereign tone.

Remove them.

8. Ensure All Claims Are Verifiable

Examples that need tightening:

• “BSV is the only blockchain capable of recording every trade on-chain.”

“BSV is the only public blockchain with demonstrated block capacity and fee structure sufficient to record every trade on-chain at sub-cent cost.”
