⚡ The Core Logic Behind DEX Trading

DEXs (like Uniswap, PancakeSwap, Curve) rely on Automated Market Makers (AMMs) — algorithmic smart contracts that replace traditional order books.

---

🧩 1. Liquidity Pools (The Foundation)

Instead of matching traders with each other, DEXs use pools of tokens deposited by liquidity providers (LPs).

Example:
A pool might contain:

• 100 ETH
• 300,000 USDT


Anyone can trade against this pool.

LPs earn:

• A share of trading fees
• Sometimes additional incentives


---

🔢 2. Pricing Logic: The Constant Product Formula

Most DEXs (like Uniswap V2) use the famous formula:

x \cdot y = k


Where:

• \(x\) = amount of Token A in the pool
• \(y\) = amount of Token B in the pool
• \(k\) = constant (cannot change)


This means:

• Every trade changes the balance of the pool
• The price adjusts automatically
• Larger trades move the price more (slippage)


This is why AMMs are called “automated market makers” — the math is the market maker.

---

🔄 3. How a Trade Actually Happens

Let’s say a trader swaps ETH → USDT:

1. Trader sends ETH into the pool
2. The pool releases USDT back
3. The ratio of ETH/USDT changes
4. The AMM recalculates the new price
5. A fee (e.g., 0.3%) is taken and distributed to LPs


No matching engine.
No counterparty.
Just math + liquidity.

---

📉 4. Slippage (Price Impact)

Because the pool must keep \(x \cdot y = k\), large trades move the price.

• Small trade → tiny price change
• Large trade → big price change


This is why deep liquidity = better prices.

---

💸 5. Fees and Incentives

DEXs charge a fee on every swap (e.g., 0.3%).
This fee is:

• Added to the pool
• Distributed to LPs


This creates a self-sustaining liquidity economy.

---

🧠 6. Arbitrage Keeps Prices Accurate

AMMs don’t know the “real” market price.
Arbitrage traders fix this:

• If ETH is cheaper on Uniswap than Binance → arbitrageurs buy on Uniswap and sell on Binance
• This pushes the AMM price back to equilibrium


Arbitrage is essential to AMM health.

---

🧬 7. Variants of AMM Logic

Different DEXs use different formulas:

DEX Type	Formula	Purpose	
Uniswap V2	\(x \cdot y = k\)	General trading	
Curve	Hybrid stable-swap curve	Low slippage for stablecoins	
Balancer	Weighted pools	Multi-asset pools (e.g., 80/20)	
Uniswap V3	Concentrated liquidity	LPs choose price ranges	


---

🛡️ 8. Risks Built Into AMM Logic

• Impermanent loss (LPs lose value when prices diverge)
• Slippage (large trades move price)
• Front-running / MEV (bots reorder transactions)


These are structural to AMM design.
